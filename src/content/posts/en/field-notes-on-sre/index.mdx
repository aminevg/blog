Some loose notes from a decade of pager duty at 3am, in no particular order.

## 1. SLOs before dashboards

Dashboards without SLOs are aquariums. Pretty to watch, unclear what to do.
Pick a handful of user-facing indicators. Set budgets. Let the budget decide
whether to ship or shore up.

## 2. Runbooks decay faster than code

Assume every runbook is wrong. Annotate them with a last-verified date. If
nobody has touched a runbook in six months, it probably lies.

## 3. Toil is a liquid

It fills the container you give it. Cap the container — if 30% of your week
goes to toil, own that number and defend it publicly.
